Research

Since coming to Princeton I have mostly been working on problems in large-scale atmospheric dynamics. The dynamics of the troposphere can be separated quite cleanly (at least outside the tropics) into the problem of understanding the zonally-symmetric circulation and the problem of understanding the zonally-asymmetric circulation. The zonally-symmetric circulation is dominated by the eddy-driven jet, whereas studies of the zonally-asymmetric circulation generally focus on stationary waves. I have worked on both problems, mainly using idealized models such as the Phillips 2-layer QG model and the GFDL dry dynamical core.
